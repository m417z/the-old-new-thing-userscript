  8. Nick Lamb says:

    You can buy ADSL on its own in the UK, but the people who sell it (e.g. A&A) are all telecoms providers aimed at businesses, and so their prices are higher (service is better too) – you won’t save any money.

    If you think about it that makes sense. BT’s line rental covers the (by no means trivial) cost of actually maintaining the final mile copper and assorted infrastructure. So a provider that doesn’t make you buy a BT line has to pay BT’s engineers for this same service anyway or else hire all their own engineers at each location they serve.

    The core costs are network infrastructure and the final mile. The final mile cost is fixed, because it consists of trained men in the rain and cold rewiring things in coffins under the street or climbing up poles and trying to make stuff work. If you cut that budget, the service becomes unreliable. The network infrastructure cost is determined by peak load on the network, which explains why "unlimited usage" plans have the same bogus small print as "lifetime" offers. Every customer making above average use is costing the provider money and making service worse for the other users.

  9. Nick Lamb says:
    August 6, 2008 at 7:10 am

    Oh, and 0845 and 0870 numbers haven’t been cheap for years. They’re pinned to the "standard" local and national UK rate for calls, but no-one pays standard rate any more due to discounts. Once upon a time 0845 numbers came at a premium to cover the lost revenue compared to a national call, but today’s telcos actually /pay you/ to run an 0845 number because they make so much money at the "standard" rate.

    The watchdog has been slowly working out a plan to get rid of all 08 prefixes except 0800 – in favour of new pseudo-geographic numbers. These numbers (prefix 03) are already available and will have to be treated like ordinary geographic (01 02) numbers by providers e.g. for discount and billing purposes, but like 08 series numbers they won’t have an area code and will be portable across the entire network.

    There’s a web site saynoto0870.com which can tell you an ordinary geographic number that will connect the same call cheaper (and from your mobile too). Not every number has a geographic equivalent but most do because you can’t call 08 series numbers from outside the UK normally, so customers abroad need a number.
