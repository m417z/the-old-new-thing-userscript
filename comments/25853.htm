    Sounds like an interesting ride.

    Here, in Ottawa, Ontario, we have a yearly ride called the Rideau Lakes Cycle Tour, From Ottawa, Ontario to Kingston Ontario on a Saturday in June, and back again on Sunday.   There are four variations on the ride.  The major one, "The Classic" fully supported, covers about 180 km each way, Ottawa-Perth-Kingston and back.   A second route which is somewhat flatter is "The Cruise" Ottawa-Merrickville-Kingston, roughly the same distance.  A third option has people leaving their luggage at Perth (halfway), and departing from there.  A dedicated truck hauls luggage from Perth to Kingston (a Tractor Trailer serves for the people who leave from Carleton University in Ottawa).

    The fourth option is "the Challenge", unsupported, 225 km each way, swinging farther west before arriving at Queens University in Kingston.

    The logistics of keeping 2000 riders safely on the road on four different routes has been developed by the Ottawa Bicycle Club since the early 1970’s
