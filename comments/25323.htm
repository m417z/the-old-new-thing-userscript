    So, Microsoft first lets everyone go around and use windows.open() however they please (but they might have to get a windows.open permit first), and if they happen to abuse that liberty, Microsoft bans them from using windows.open() completely?  No, windows.open() was changed to prevent many of the bad acts of the bad people (banned all the handguns and machine guns*) and that can end up hurting people who weren’t abusing windows.open().  The fact that people can create a bubble to reverse that (declare one’s home a soverign nation and allow all types of guns to be used**) doesn’t negate that windows.open() (gun law) was changed, effecting not just the "bad people" but also the "good people".

    *Not a great analogy, but they’re the most commonly demonized forms of guns.

    **And this is one reason the gun analogy breaks down.  Microsoft isn’t the law, so extrapolating "bans" is incredibly difficult; also, Microsoft does go out of its way to allow for people to counter changes so programs don’t break, while governments are more willing to make sweeping changes that hurt some people because those who are hurt represent a minority (ie, they’re not as strictly driven by monetary considerations).  Now, the externalities of windows.open() changes and stricter gun laws could be something to compare.  But, that’s merely a small section of the bigger picture.  In short, not everything can be turned into a gun or car analogy.
