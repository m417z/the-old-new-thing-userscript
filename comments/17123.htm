    To be very honest, though, the living by themselves when in 30’s may only apply in western societies or wealthy countries. In many Asian countries for example, many singles (and sometimes even married couples!) still live with their parents. The reasons are not outside imaginations:

    1. In some Eastern cultures, living in family settings is a better virtue than living by themselves.

    2. Cost-saving. Take Hong Kong, my birth-place, as an example. A 300-feet unit can cost about HK$2mil (round that to about US$250K), while a friend of mine being a teacher (considered good paid in HK) had an income equivalent to US$30K/yr. Rentals are pretty expensive too. It is far cheaper to live with your parents. (By the same argument, buying a car in HK, even when translated to US currency, still costs 3-4 times more than the exact same model in USA.)

    3. Even when living with parents, many young people (singles or couples) bear the financial responsibilities, because they are better paid (or their parents have retired). I’ve even seen 3-generation families living under the same unit way smaller than 1000 sq. fts.

    So, while the "Being an adult is being self-supportive" argument may be true in USA, I consider this as culture-specific, as in Eastern cultures, being an adult takes further as "taking responsibilities of supporting your family members", from financial suitations to daily lives, because quite some singles and couples choose to live with their parents as their elderly parents need physical help.
